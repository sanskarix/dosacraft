# Dosa Craft SEO Content Blueprint

## Phase 1. Brand Voice Analysis

### Brand Voice

Dosa Craft should sound like a modern neighborhood food brand that respects tradition but never feels old-fashioned. The voice should feel:

- warm and inviting
- food-first, not brand-first
- confident without sounding premium for the sake of it
- rooted in craft, freshness, and consistency
- playful when talking about fusion dishes, grounded when talking about quality

### Brand Personality

- curious and creative
- approachable and family-friendly
- proudly South Indian in foundation
- contemporary in presentation
- consistent and dependable

### Core Values

- authentic dosa foundations
- fresh ingredients
- reliable taste across outlets
- visual appeal without gimmickry
- welcoming dine-in experience
- innovation that still respects the original dish

### Emotional Story

The brand is built around a simple emotional truth: people already love dosa, but they also want surprise, variety, and a place that feels current. Dosa Craft exists to keep the comfort of a familiar dish while making it feel exciting again.

### What Makes Dosa Craft Distinct

- it treats dosa as a craft category, not just a menu item
- it combines classic South Indian structure with Indore-friendly flavor curiosity
- it offers both familiar comfort and conversation-starting fusion plates
- it positions dosa as an everyday meal, a group outing, a breakfast option, and a street-food indulgence

### Writing Voice To Use On The Website

Write like a host who knows the menu well and genuinely wants people to drop in. Keep sentences short. Use food words people can picture. Prioritize taste, texture, freshness, and ease. Avoid startup language, corporate abstractions, and empty claims like "world-class" or "revolutionary."

## Phase 2. Website and SEO Audit

### What The Business Offers

Dosa Craft offers South Indian and fusion dosa dishes in Indore, with a wide range of classics, signature creations, kids' options, Jain variants, and uttapam.

### Target Audience

- Indore residents searching for dosa, South Indian food, breakfast, or casual dining
- students and office-goers looking for filling, affordable meals
- families and groups who want menu variety
- tourists searching for notable local food spots
- customers searching by intent like "best dosa in Indore" or "dosa near me"

### Market Positioning

Dosa Craft sits between a traditional South Indian restaurant and a modern street-food-led fusion brand. That is a strong position, but the current site does not express it clearly enough.

### Short SEO Audit

#### Strengths

- multiple pages already exist for homepage, menu, about, and contact
- menu breadth creates strong keyword coverage potential
- visual food-led positioning fits discovery intent well

#### Weaknesses

- messaging is generic and often reads like a brand brief instead of customer-facing copy
- local SEO signals are weak or inconsistent
- page copy underuses high-intent local phrases naturally
- content hierarchy is broad but not tightly aligned to search intent
- outlet information is inconsistent across site/public references, which weakens entity trust
- canonical tags are missing
- Open Graph and rich local business/schema coverage are weak
- some social/contact links are placeholders
- AI-search-ready FAQ structure is limited

### High-Risk Local SEO Issue

Current site content and public references do not clearly align on exact outlet/location details. Before scaling SEO, Dosa Craft should lock the official business name, address, phone, opening hours, menu URL, and Google Maps URL for each outlet.

## Phase 9. Technical SEO Recommendations

### Immediate Technical Priorities

- add canonical tags to all pages
- add unique Open Graph and Twitter metadata
- add `Restaurant` and `LocalBusiness` schema with exact outlet data
- add dedicated locations page with one section or URL per outlet
- create `sitemap.xml`
- create `robots.txt`
- replace placeholder social links and incomplete contact data
- standardize NAP data across website, Google Business Profile, maps, and food directories

### Canonical Tags

Use one canonical per page, for example:

- homepage: `https://dosacraft.in/`
- menu: `https://dosacraft.in/menu`
- about: `https://dosacraft.in/about`
- contact: `https://dosacraft.in/contact`

### Schema Markup

Use:

- `Restaurant` with `servesCuisine`, `priceRange`, `menu`, `telephone`, `address`, `image`
- `openingHoursSpecification`
- `sameAs` for social profiles
- `hasMap`
- `FAQPage` on homepage/contact

### Google Business Optimization

- one verified profile per outlet
- identical business name format across all profiles
- same phone and address formatting everywhere
- upload real food, storefront, seating, and counter photos
- keep menu, hours, holiday hours, and attributes updated
- actively collect and respond to reviews mentioning signature items and locations

### Sitemap and Robots

- include all indexable pages in `sitemap.xml`
- exclude duplicate, test, and placeholder URLs
- ensure `robots.txt` allows crawl for CSS, JS, and images

### Alt Tag Improvements

Current images should use descriptive alt text such as:

- `Cheese Volcano dosa at Dosa Craft in Indore`
- `Masala dosa served at Dosa Craft restaurant`
- `Family dining area at Dosa Craft Indore outlet`

### Core Web Vitals Recommendations

#### LCP

- compress hero and menu images
- preload the hero image and primary fonts
- use WebP where possible

#### CLS

- set width and height for all images
- reserve space for banners, map blocks, and menu media

#### FCP

- reduce unused JS
- inline only critical CSS for above-the-fold content
- defer non-essential motion scripts
